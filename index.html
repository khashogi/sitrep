2. MILITARY OPERATIONS

Coalition — 15,000+ Targets, Marines Deploying: Hegseth announced Friday that US-Israeli forces have struck more than 15,000 targets in Iran, describing the operation as achieving "lethal effects in entirely new ways" with AI-enabled targeting. Over 90 Iranian naval vessels destroyed including all four Soleimani-class warships. Israel struck 200+ targets across western and central Iran in 24 hours — ballistic missile launchers, defense systems, weapons production sites. Israel also struck Basij checkpoints in Tehran, with ambassador Leiter confirming: "Today we hit Basij forces and roadblocks... restricting the movement of locals." Marines Expeditionary Unit (~2,500 personnel) deploying to region.

Mojtaba Khamenei — "Wounded and Disfigured": Hegseth confirmed Friday that Iran's new Supreme Leader is "wounded and likely disfigured." Reports indicate fractured foot, facial lacerations, and possible abdominal/leg injuries requiring surgery. Mojtaba has not appeared on video since succession. His first statement Thursday — read by TV anchor, not delivered personally — vowed Hormuz closure continues and attacks on US bases will intensify unless bases are closed. Trump: "I think he's damaged, but I think he's probably alive in some form." Israel's Katz warned any appointed leader will be "target for elimination."

US Aircraft Crash — 6 Killed: KC-135 Stratotanker refueling aircraft crashed in western Iraq Thursday. All 6 crew members killed. CENTCOM stated incident was "not due to hostile fire or friendly fire" and involved a second tanker that landed safely in Israel. However, Ashab al-Kahf (Iran-backed militia) claimed responsibility and threatened all French interests in Iraq would be "under fire." Total US military deaths now 13 since Feb 28.

Lebanon — "Gaza-Scale" Threat: Israel destroyed the Zrarieh Bridge over the Litani River Friday — first acknowledged strike on civilian infrastructure. Defense Minister Katz warned Lebanon faces "increasing costs through damage to infrastructure and loss of territory." Leaflets dropped on Beirut: "In light of the great success in Gaza, the newspaper of the new reality arrives to Lebanon." IDF struck central Beirut neighborhoods (Nabaa, Bourj Hammoud) for first time in this conflict. Hezbollah chief Qassem: "existential battle." 773+ killed, 800K+ displaced, 14% of Lebanon under evacuation orders.

4. CRITICAL INFRASTRUCTURE & ENERGY

Oil Market — IEA Release Fails to Calm:

  • Brent crude: $100-101/barrel Friday, up from ~$70 pre-war. Goldman Sachs forecasts 20% higher oil prices for 2026 overall, averaging $100+ in March, $85 in April before dropping to ~$70 by year-end
  • IEA record release: 400M barrels from strategic reserves — largest in agency history. US contributing 172M barrels starting next week (120 days to deliver). Prior Ukraine-era release was 182M barrels
  • Scale mismatch: 400M barrels = ~4 days global production vs. 20M bbl/day disrupted at Hormuz. Macquarie: "If that doesn't sound like much, it isn't"
  • Japan: Releasing 80M barrels (45 days of reserves). South Korea: first fuel price cap in 30 years
  • US sanctions waiver: Treasury issued 30-day license allowing purchases of Russian oil products to ease pressure
  • IEA assessment: "Largest supply disruption in the history of the global oil market." Global oil supply projected to plunge 8M bbl/d in March. LNG supply reduced 20%

Supply Chain Crises Emerging:

  • Helium shortage: Qatar (2nd largest helium producer) halted production — chipmakers have ~30-40 days inventory. If war lasts 3 months, semiconductor production impacted
  • Fertilizer crisis: Urea prices up 34% in past month. Australia imports 2/3 from Middle East, supplies last until mid-April. Farmers may shift to crops requiring less fertilizer
  • Global austerity: Pakistan 4-day work week continues. Bangladesh dimming lights, reducing travel. Philippines shortened work weeks. South Korea fuel price caps. India invoking emergency powers to divert fuel from industrial to household use

Aviation & Shipping:

  • Flight disruptions: Dubai airport damaged. Qatar airspace closed (140+ repatriation flights scheduled). 18% of world air freight capacity disappeared
  • Shipping: 16+ vessels attacked in/around Hormuz since Feb 28. US Navy unable to provide escorts (deemed too dangerous). Iraq shut port operations after tanker attacks
  • Australia: Ordered "non-essential" officials to evacuate UAE and Israel. Released 762M liters of petrol/diesel reserves
