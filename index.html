2. MILITARY OPERATIONS

US Operations

The US dropped 5,000-pound bunker-buster bombs on Iranian missile sites along the Strait of Hormuz coastline, targeting hardened underground facilities. This represents the first use of heavy penetration munitions on Hormuz-adjacent infrastructure, signaling intent to degrade Iran's ability to threaten shipping. The USS Gerald R. Ford carrier is departing theater after suffering a fire — reducing US force projection capacity in a critical phase.

Israeli Operations

Israel's decapitation campaign achieved three major kills in 48 hours: Intelligence Minister Esmail Khatib (Tuesday night), security chief Ali Larijani (Tuesday), and Basij commander Gholamreza Soleimani. Defense Minister Israel Katz announced a blanket authorization allowing military to kill "any senior Iranian official" once opportunity arises — "without the need for additional approval." This institutionalizes the assassination campaign. Israel confirmed striking the South Pars gas field "in coordination with the United States."

Iranian Operations

Iranian ballistic missile launches have declined 90% from Day 1 (350 to ~25 by Day 15). Drone launches similarly collapsed from 800+ to ~75. Analysts attribute this to arsenal depletion and strategic rationing. However, Iran's horizontal escalation doctrine remains active: attacks on Saudi Arabia (12 drones Wednesday), Kuwait, Iraq, and fresh threats against named Gulf energy facilities demonstrate willingness to expand targets as direct strike capacity diminishes. Iran is increasingly relying on "permission-based transits" through Hormuz — offering safe passage to Chinese, Indian, Pakistani vessels while maintaining the blockade on Western-linked shipping.

The dual leadership vacuum (Mojtaba incapacitated + Larijani killed) removes both the figurehead and the operational diplomat simultaneously.

4. ENERGY & INFRASTRUCTURE

South Pars Strike — Upstream Production Under Attack

The strike on South Pars marks a qualitative phase transition. This is the world's largest natural gas reserve, producing 730M cubic meters/day in 2025. Four processing sections taken offline; two major plants (~100M cubic meters/day capacity) halted to contain fires. The field supplies ~70% of Iran's domestic gas AND is the source of most of Qatar's LNG exports. Turkey also receives supply from the field. Rystad Energy warns that prolonged shutdowns can cause reservoir damage that "kills the well" — shifting recovery from weeks/months to YEARS or permanent impairment.

Iran Names Specific Gulf Targets

Within hours of South Pars strike, Iran issued explicit warnings:

  • Saudi Arabia: Samref Refinery, Jubail Petrochemical Complex
  • UAE: Al Hosn Gas Field
  • Qatar: Mesaieed Petrochemical Complex, Mesaieed Holding, Ras Laffan Refinery

This is operational signaling, not general deterrence. The language marked a shift from blanket threats to specific facility targeting.

Oil Prices

Brent spiked to $109.75 intraday, closing around $105-108 — up 6%+ on the South Pars news. Oil is up ~50% since February 28. US gas prices: $3.84/gallon national average (+29% in 18 days), with California/Hawaii/Washington above $5. JP Morgan flags "oil price misalignment" — Brent/WTI are Atlantic Basin benchmarks cushioned by regional inventories, while Middle Eastern benchmarks (Dubai, Oman) reflect true physical dislocation. "The apparent stability in Brent and WTI should not be taken as evidence of ample global supply."

Hormuz Status

Approximately 90 ships have transited since war began (89 from March 1-15), vs. 100+ per DAY pre-war. Traffic down 95%+. 21+ tankers transited, mostly Iran-affiliated or "dark" vessels. Pakistan, India secured diplomatic passage for specific vessels. Iran is creating a de facto "permission corridor" — Chinese/Indian/Pakistani flagged ships declaring their affiliation via AIS to gain safe passage. Iran still exporting 16M+ barrels since March via its own channels. The stranglehold on Western shipping remains total.

It's a good thing that he's out."
