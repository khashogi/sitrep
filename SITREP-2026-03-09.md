## 2. MILITARY OPERATIONS

**Coalition Campaign — New Phase (Energy Targeting):**
Israel launched "wide-scale" strikes on Iranian regime targets in Tehran and "additional areas" on Sunday, including IRGC aerospace force headquarters and multiple command centers and missile launch sites. The energy infrastructure campaign that began Friday-Saturday has now destroyed or damaged oil storage depots in Karaj, Shahran, Aghdasieh, and Shahr-e Rey (all in and around Tehran). CNN teams in Tehran reported oily, blackened rain falling on the city Sunday morning — visible evidence of the scale of fuel facility fires. A senior Iranian official described the strikes as the opening of a "new phase" of the war.

Trump stated he has not yet decided whether to strike Iran's nuclear facilities or oil export infrastructure directly. Energy Secretary Chris Wright told CNN the US does not plan to strike Iranian energy infrastructure — but confirmed the Israeli strikes on Iranian oil depots were partially unexpected in scope, with CNN reporting some Trump administration officials "surprised" by the breadth of the weekend strikes and concerned about the political implications of rising energy costs ahead of midterms.

Hegseth suggested the campaign could run "as long as eight weeks." A senior Israeli military officer (speaking anonymously) told NPR that Israel needs "three more weeks" to accomplish its objectives — setting up a potential divergence between US political timelines and Israeli operational needs.

**Uranium Seizure Mission Under Discussion:**
Bloomberg reports Trump is actively contemplating a special forces mission to physically enter Iran and seize approximately 441 kg of highly enriched uranium (HEU) — only a short technical step from weapons-grade — which was reportedly moved to undisclosed locations before the June 2025 US nuclear strikes. If confirmed, this would represent a major escalation beyond airstrikes into ground combat on Iranian soil. The mission has reportedly not been ordered.

**Lebanon — Ground War Expanding:**
IDF confirmed first soldier deaths in Lebanon — two killed Sunday in southern Lebanon as fighting deepened. Israeli forces struck Hezbollah targets in Dahiyeh (Beirut's southern suburbs) and the Bekaa Valley. Human Rights Watch confirmed Israel used white phosphorus in residential areas of southern Lebanon, a violation of international humanitarian law. IDF killed five IRGC commanders based in Lebanon. Lebanese parliament voted to extend its own term by two years, indicating civilian government has effectively suspended normal political operations for the duration.

**IRIS Dena Sinking Confirmed:**
Iran confirmed 104 crew killed and 32 wounded in the US strike on the Iranian warship IRIS Dena in the Indian Ocean last week. The vessel sinking has been acknowledged by both sides with contested narrative about whether the ship was armed.
